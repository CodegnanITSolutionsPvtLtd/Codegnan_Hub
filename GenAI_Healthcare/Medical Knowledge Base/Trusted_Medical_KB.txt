Trusted Medical Knowledge Base (For Educational Use Only)

Hemoglobin:
Hemoglobin is a protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen throughout the body.
Normal range for adult males is approximately 13.0–17.0 g/dL.
Normal range for adult females is approximately 12.0–15.0 g/dL.
Low or high hemoglobin values should be discussed with a qualified doctor.

White Blood Cell (WBC) Count:
White blood cell count measures the number of white blood cells in the blood.
Normal range is approximately 4,000–11,000 cells per microliter.
Abnormal WBC values may indicate infection or inflammation.
Consult a doctor for interpretation.

Platelet Count:
Platelets help with blood clotting and prevent excessive bleeding.
Normal range is approximately 150,000–450,000 per microliter.
Low or high platelet counts require medical evaluation.
